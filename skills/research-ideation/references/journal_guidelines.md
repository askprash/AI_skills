# Target-Journal Author & Figure Guidelines

Reference for the two journal families this researcher publishes in. Load the relevant section before drafting or formatting a manuscript. Verify against the live author page before submission — specs change.

> **Two families, two cultures.** Atmospheric/environmental venues are mostly author-year, uncertainty-heavy, and require implications + data-availability sections. AIAA engineering venues are numbered-bracket, require a Nomenclature section and formal V&V, and prefer dual SI/English units. Don't cross-contaminate conventions.

---

## FAMILY A — Atmospheric / Environmental

### Energy & Environmental Science (RSC)
- **Article types:** Communication (~4 printed pages, high-impact), Full Paper (8–15 pp), Review, Perspective.
- **Abstract:** unstructured, ~150–250 words.
- **Structure:** flexible IMRAD, no mandated headings; extensive SI expected.
- **Figures:** TIFF at production (**600 dpi min — higher than everyone else**); EPS/PDF/PNG accepted at submission. Single col ~86 mm, double ~174 mm. Color free.
- **TOC graphic mandatory** (~8 × 4 cm). Frame the energy-transition relevance.
- **References:** numbered superscript, RSC style; DOIs included.
- **Data:** mandatory Data Availability Statement; deposit in Zenodo/Figshare. ORCID for corresponding author.
- **Gotcha:** 600 dpi requirement; "Communication" = 4 pages, not a short letter.
- URL: https://www.rsc.org/journals-books-databases/about-journals/energy-environmental-science/

### Environmental Research Letters (IOP)
- **Article types:** Letter (**4,000-word max**, the primary research format — a *full* article, not a brief comm), Perspective (2,000 words, no abstract, ≤2 display items), Topical Review.
- **Abstract:** unstructured, ~100–200 words.
- **Figures:** EPS/PDF preferred (vector); TIFF/PNG accepted. Single col ~85 mm, double ~150 mm. **8–12 pt fonts at final size.** **Colorblind accessibility enforced by reviewers** — never encode information by color alone.
- **References:** **author-year (Harvard)**; titles included.
- **Data:** Data Availability Statement required; FAIR/repository expected.
- **Unique:** a **Justification Statement** (separate from cover letter, sent to reviewers) is mandatory — explains why the work demands urgent global attention. Commonly missed.
- URL: https://publishingsupport.iopscience.iop.org/journals/environmental-research-letters/

### EGU — Atmospheric Chemistry and Physics (ACP) & Atmospheric Measurement Techniques (AMT), via Copernicus
- **Routing:** AMT for instruments/retrievals/technique; ACP for atmospheric science interpretation.
- **Article types:** Research Article (no length cap), ACP Letter (<2,500 words, <200-word abstract, 4-week discussion), Measurement Report (title starts "Measurement report:", data DOI required at submission), Technical Note (title starts "Technical note:"), Review (pre-approval).
- **Abstract:** **structured by content** (7 elements: topic+importance, current understanding, gap, objectives, approach, main quantitative results, implications), ≤250 words.
- **Structure:** flexible, but the **concluding section is mandatory and must state atmospheric/climate implications** — editors check for it.
- **Figures (Copernicus, all EGU):** PDF/PS/EPS preferred (vector), 300 dpi min raster. Min width 8 cm; single ~8.3 cm, double ~17 cm. Embed fonts (Arial/Helvetica). Combine all panels into one file. Place figures in text near first mention. **Colorblind-safe palettes required.** Files named fig01.pdf…
- **References:** author-year; titles included; alphabetical list.
- **Review model:** **interactive public review** — preprint posted publicly on submission (no embargo possible); discussion paper has a separate DOI.
- **Data/code:** separate Data Availability and Code/Model Availability sections; Zenodo/PANGAEA/etc.; CC BY preferred.
- URL: https://www.atmospheric-chemistry-and-physics.net/policies/guidelines_for_authors.html · https://publications.copernicus.org/for_authors/manuscript_preparation.html

### AGU — JGR: Atmospheres, GRL, Earth's Future, AGU Advances
- **Publication Units (PU):** 1 PU = 500 words OR 1 figure OR 1 table. **GRL ≤ 12 PU** (papers over are returned unreviewed); JGR:A / Earth's Future / AGU Advances ≤ 25 PU; Reviews ≤ 50. *Budget figures first.* (e.g. GRL with 6 figures ⇒ only ~3,000 words incl. abstract.)
- **Abstract:** unstructured. **GRL ≤ 150 words** (strict); others ~250.
- **Mandatory extras (all AGU):** **3 Key Points** (≤140 chars each); **Plain Language Summary** (≤200 words, no jargon/acronyms/equations, editorially reviewed) for GRL/JGR:A; **CRediT** author contributions; **Open Research section** listing data + software.
- **Figures:** EPS/TIFF/JPG/PDF; 300–600 ppi. ~89 mm / 133 mm / 178 mm equivalents. **8 pt min at final size.** Panels combined; sub-panels labeled 1a, 1b; captions in manuscript only (not in figure files). Color free.
- **References:** author-year; dataset/software DOIs cited in the list.
- **Data:** strictest of the set — **"available upon request" is explicitly prohibited**; repository DOI required.
- **Routing:** comprehensive study → JGR:A; single breakthrough → GRL.
- URL: https://www.agu.org/publications/authors/journals/text-graphics-requirements · https://www.agu.org/publications/authors/journals/data-software-for-authors

### Nature, Nature Climate Change, Nature Geoscience, Nature Communications, Science
| | Nature Article | NClimate / NGeo Article | Nat. Commun. | Science Res. Article |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Main text | ~2,500 or ~4,300 words | ≤3,000 words | ≤~5,000 words | 6,000–8,000 (initial); ~3,000 (published) |
| Abstract | summary para ≤~200 w | ≤150 w (NCC) / ≤200 w (NGeo) | ≤200 w | ≤250 w |
| Display items | 4–6 (+≤10 Extended Data) | ≤6 (+Extended Data) | ≤10 (≤4 if <2,000 w) | 3–5 (5–8 initial) |
| Refs | ~50 | ~50 | ~70 | numbered |
- **Abstract formula (Nature family):** 2–3 sentences background → rationale → "**Here we show…**" → implications.
- **Structure:** Intro (bold first para, no heading) → body w/ subheads → **Methods online-only** (Nature/NCC/NGeo; *Science Methods ARE in print*). Results+Discussion often combined in short papers.
- **Figures:** 90 mm / 180 mm (Science 89/127/185 mm); **300 dpi**; **5–7 pt sans-serif labels** (Nature) / ~7 pt (Science); EPS/TIFF/PDF final; scale bars + defined error bars required.
- **References:** **numbered sequential**, superscript; article titles included for long-form.
- **Data/Code:** mandatory separate availability statements; accession numbers; code at acceptance.
- **Gotchas:** Nature Methods not in print (Science's are); Science published version is far shorter than the initial submission.

## FAMILY B — Aerospace Engineering (AIAA)
*All AIAA technical journals share one rulebook. Per-venue differences are scope + the Journal-of-Aircraft "Design Forum" type. See `aiaa_engineering_style.md` for full style detail.*

- **Venues:** AIAA Journal (broad), Journal of Aircraft (configs/MDO/aero; **Design Forum** = case studies/methods, 10–12k words, *no peer review*, fast), Journal of Propulsion and Power (cycles/turbomachinery/combustion/electric propulsion), JGCD (GN&C, requires theory + validation), AIAA SciTech/Aviation conference papers (two-column template, ≤~25 pp, program-committee review).
- **Article types:** Full-Length Paper (10–12k words, abstract 100–200 w), Survey (18–20k, no abstract, bio required), Technical Note (2.5–3.5k, no abstract, editor review), Comment (≤1,200 w).
- **Abstract:** one paragraph, **100–200 words, third person**, no citations / no figure refs / no acronyms; keywords + key findings in first two sentences.
- **Structure:** Abstract → **Nomenclature** (symbols only, alphabetical, Roman→Greek→subscripts; *if used, do not redefine symbols in text*; acronyms go in text not nomenclature) → Introduction → descriptively-titled method/analysis sections → Results & Discussion → Conclusions (discuss findings, **introduce nothing new, cite nothing new**) → Acknowledgments → References.
- **Figures:** **single col 3.25 in, full width 7 in**; **line art ≥600 dpi, photos ≥300 dpi**; **≥8 pt fonts at column width**; min line weight 0.28 pt; PDF (line art) / PNG / TIFF; **PowerPoint and GIF rejected**; captions ≤~20–25 words; sub-figures a), b)…; no company logos. Prefer perceptually-uniform colormaps (not jet); design for grayscale.
- **References:** **numbered brackets** [4], [8–10], [11, p. 250]; **all authors listed (no "et al." in the list)**; full (unabbreviated) journal/book titles; DOI as https URL; conference papers cite "AIAA Paper YYYY-NNNN."
- **Units:** **dual SI + English** preferred throughout.
- **V&V (editorial policy — desk-reject if missing):** CFD needs code verification, **grid-independence study with ≥3 meshes and GCI**, iterative + (if unsteady) temporal convergence, validation vs experiment with uncertainty bands; experiments need bias+precision uncertainty combined and reported at a stated coverage factor (e.g. ±X at 95%).
- **Templates:** Word `arc.aiaa.org/r/journalwordtemplate`; LaTeX (Overleaf) `overleaf.com/latex/templates/.../mqqbqqvyhtwm`; conference LaTeX `.../rsssbwthkptn`.
- URL: https://aiaa.org/publications/journals/Journal-Author/ · figures: https://aiaa.org/publications/journals/Journal-Author/Guidelines-for-Journal-Figures-and-Tables/ · refs: https://aiaa.org/publications/journals/reference-style-and-format/

## Top cross-journal "gotchas"
1. E&ES wants **600 dpi**; everyone else (raster) 300.
2. ERL **Justification Statement** ≠ cover letter, and goes to reviewers.
3. AGU bans **"available upon request"** — repository DOI required.
4. EGU posts your **preprint publicly on submission** — no embargo.
5. ACP **conclusions must state climate/atmospheric implications**.
6. AGU **PU budget** is arithmetic — each figure = 1 PU; GRL ≤ 12.
7. Nature **Methods are online-only**; Science's are in print.
8. Citation split: **author-year** (ERL, EGU, AGU) vs **numbered** (RSC, Nature, Science, AIAA).
9. AIAA: **no "et al." in the reference list**, no abbreviated journal names, **grid-convergence/GCI mandatory** for CFD.
